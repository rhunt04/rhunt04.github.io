I am (2021-) a post-doctoral research associate at the Engineering Department of Lancaster University.

From 2019-2021, I worked full-time as a Software Engineer for a control and automation company in the North West.

From 2015-2019, I earned a PhD in the Lancaster University Department of Physics. I was then a member of the Condensed Matter Theory group (as part of the Graphene NOWNANO CDT; and under the supervision of Neil Drummond). My thesis is freely available.

Currently, I am working with Sam Murphy in the Lancaster University Engineering Department on modelling radiation damage in materials. We want to understand the role that electrons play in the propagation of radiation damage cascades.

During my PhD, I used Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods to understand (i) excitations in materials from first-principles, and (ii) the dynamics of charge-carrier complexes in semiconductors (at a model level). To this end, I spent ~4 years as an active user and contributor to the CASINO QMC code.

Details of my publications are available on this site, and at links in the page footer. Details of awards I have received are available elsewhere (see e.g. LinkedIn).

Research data from my time as a PhD student are freely available.

Various social links are in the page footer.

Interested parties are welcome to ask me questions about my research, or indeed to request data or copies of manuscripts from me. I will acquiesce to most reasonable requests.
